About MRT Energy

Our Story

Founded in 2012, MRT Group (Pty) Ltd began as a family-owned business rooted in innovation, trust, and sustainability. The name "MRT" represents the initials of the founder's children — Mmanake (M), Rapelo (R), and Tlaitirang (T) — with "M" also symbolizing Mamotsekola, the founder's wife, reflecting the family's unity and legacy.

Over the past decade, MRT has evolved into a leading player in soil stabilization and road construction technologies, pioneering the adoption of Permazyme across Africa.

Vision

To be a globally recognized leader in sustainable infrastructure solutions — driving innovation in soil stabilization, road construction, and geotechnical excellence across Africa and beyond. By 2035, MRT will have expanded into at least 20 African countries, established regional innovation hubs, and partnered with governments, municipalities, and industries to deliver environmentally responsible, cost-effective infrastructure that transforms communities and enhances mobility.

Mission

To provide innovative, eco-friendly, and cost-effective road construction and soil stabilization solutions that empower our clients to build durable infrastructure using locally available materials. We serve our customers by combining advanced technologies such as Permazyme with engineering expertise, hands-on training, and long-term technical support — ensuring every project delivers measurable quality, sustainability, and value.

Permazyme Technology

Revolutionary Enzyme-Based Soil Stabilization

Permazyme is a cutting-edge, bio-enzymatic soil stabilization technology that transforms naturally available soils into durable, high-performance road surfaces. This innovative solution represents the future of sustainable infrastructure development.

What is Permazyme?

Permazyme is an organic, non-toxic enzyme-based liquid additive that modifies soil properties at a molecular level. When mixed with soil and water, it catalyzes chemical reactions that bind soil particles together, creating a strong, water-resistant, and durable pavement layer.

How It Works

  • Molecular Bonding: Enzymes catalyze reactions between soil particles
  • Water Resistance: Creates hydrophobic barrier preventing water penetration
  • Density Increase: Compacts soil to optimal density levels
  • Long-term Stability: Maintains structural integrity for years

Key Benefits

  • Cost Reduction: Up to 40% savings compared to traditional methods
  • Use Local Materials: Utilizes in-situ soils, reducing transport costs
  • Fast Construction: Rapid curing time accelerates project completion
  • Eco-Friendly: Non-toxic, biodegradable, zero carbon emissions
  • Durability: Superior resistance to weathering and traffic loads
  • Minimal Maintenance: Reduced long-term maintenance requirements

Applications

Road Construction

Primary and secondary roads, highways, and arterial routes

Urban Development

Municipal streets, residential areas, parking lots

Industrial Sites

Mining haul roads, industrial yards, heavy-duty pavements

Airfields

Airport runways, taxiways, aprons

Agricultural Roads

Farm access roads, rural infrastructure

Slope Stabilization

Erosion control, embankments, soil reinforcement
